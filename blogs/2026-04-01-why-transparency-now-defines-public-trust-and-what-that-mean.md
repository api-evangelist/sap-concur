<p>It&nbsp;rarely starts with a headline. Just a small moment&nbsp;that’s&nbsp;easy to miss, until it&nbsp;isn’t.&nbsp;</p>

<p>A report request comes in.&nbsp;A number&nbsp;doesn’t&nbsp;quite reconcile.&nbsp;A leader asks a simple question that&nbsp;doesn’t&nbsp;have a simple answer.&nbsp;</p>

<p lang="EN-US">Transparency looks the same on the surface, but the risk behind it is different.&nbsp;You’ve&nbsp;likely experienced&nbsp;some version of this:&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>Pulling data from three different systems just to answer one question&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Double-checking numbers you already checked because the stakes are too high&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Pausing before sending a report, wondering if anything could be misinterpreted&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<p>These&nbsp;aren’t&nbsp;failures.&nbsp;They’re&nbsp;signals.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Signals that the expectations around&nbsp;trust and&nbsp;transparency&nbsp;in the public sector&nbsp;have&nbsp;changed&nbsp;faster than most&nbsp;spend management&nbsp;systems have.&nbsp;</p>

<p>And increasingly, those expectations are tied directly to risk.&nbsp;</p>

<p>In fact, cybersecurity has rapidly risen to become the&nbsp;top external concern for finance leaders, surpassing economic conditions and geopolitical pressures.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Because when visibility increases, so does accountability.&nbsp;</p>

<h2>The Shift No One Announced, But Everyone Feels&nbsp;</h2>

<p>Transparency&nbsp;didn’t&nbsp;become central to public trust overnight.&nbsp;It happened gradually:&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>More oversight&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Faster access to information&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Higher expectations from the public,&nbsp;government, and regulators&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<p lang="EN-US">Then, over time, it became clear: managing spend&nbsp;wasn’t&nbsp;enough. We had to be ready to explain it, too.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Across state and local government, higher education, utilities, healthcare, aerospace and defense, and K-12 schools, this shift&nbsp;shows up in&nbsp;the same way:&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p lang="EN-US">The friction of every day, mundane work that bogs us down and prevents us from being more strategic.&nbsp;</p>

<h2>What It Feels Like on the Inside&nbsp;</h2>

<p>At&nbsp;<a href="https://www.concur.com/resource-center/case-studies/coulson-aviation">Coulson Aviation</a>, a global aerial firefighting organization, finance teams were navigating rapid growth while supporting crews&nbsp;operating&nbsp;across multiple countries.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Behind the scenes, their processes looked familiar:&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>Invoice approvals happening over email&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Receipts sent from the field, often&nbsp;delayed&nbsp;or lost&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Teams working weekends to keep up with manual workloads&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<p>As their operations scaled,&nbsp;from a handful of corporate cards to hundreds,&nbsp;those gaps became harder to manage:&nbsp;</p>

<blockquote>
<p><em>“Our processes were very manual and prone to&nbsp;error. The&nbsp;team&nbsp;didn’t&nbsp;have the tools to do their jobs in a consistent, repeatable way.”&nbsp;&nbsp;</em></p>
</blockquote>

<p>And in an environment where teams are focused on mission-critical work,&nbsp;like wildfire response,&nbsp;those inefficiencies&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;just slow finance down.&nbsp;</p>

<p>They pull attention away from what matters most.&nbsp;</p>

<p>In other highly regulated industries,&nbsp;this&nbsp;can also&nbsp;look like:&nbsp;</p>

<ol>
	<li><strong>A utilities finance team preparing a regulatory submission...&nbsp;</strong><br />
	They know the data is there, but it lives across systems that&nbsp;weren’t&nbsp;designed to speak to each other.&nbsp;So,&nbsp;they&nbsp;reconcile,&nbsp;and&nbsp;re-reconcile...&nbsp;Until they&nbsp;build a&nbsp;version&nbsp;they can finally stand behind.&nbsp;<br />
	&nbsp;</li>
	<li><strong>A healthcare&nbsp;administrator in&nbsp;mid-audit...&nbsp;</strong><br />
	They&nbsp;aren't&nbsp;worried about whether&nbsp;spending&nbsp;policies were followed.&nbsp;They're&nbsp;worried about how long it will take to prove&nbsp;that&nbsp;they were.&nbsp;Did the HCP attach the receipt? Where is the approval documented? Is the expense correctly coded?&nbsp;<br />
	&nbsp;</li>
	<li><strong>A higher education team managing decentralized spend...&nbsp;</strong><br />
	They’re balancing grants, departments,&nbsp;travel&nbsp;and compliance requirements without a single, unified view&nbsp;or&nbsp;even&nbsp;sometimes&nbsp;the authority to enforce one.&nbsp;</li>
</ol>

<p>None of these situations are&nbsp;unusual.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>In fact,&nbsp;they’re&nbsp;often the result of manual processes and disconnected systems that reduce visibility and make accountability&nbsp;even&nbsp;harder to&nbsp;demonstrate.&nbsp;</p>

<p>And over time these moments accumulate.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Not into failure, but friction.&nbsp;</p>

<h2>The Compounding Effect of Friction&nbsp;</h2>

<p>Individually, each delay or workaround seems manageable&nbsp;in&nbsp;the moment.&nbsp;</p>

<p>But together, they create something heavier:&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>Slower response times&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
	<li>More manual validation&nbsp;and risk of errors&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Valuable institutional knowledge that&nbsp;isn’t&nbsp;always captured or scalable&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Greater exposure when questions come in unexpectedly&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<p>And&nbsp;here’s&nbsp;where the shift becomes&nbsp;even&nbsp;more subtle, and more important:&nbsp;</p>

<p>Transparency&nbsp;isn’t&nbsp;lost in one moment.&nbsp;<br />
It’s diluted over many.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Over time, these moments accumulate.&nbsp;Small points of&nbsp;friction turn into frustration, and frustration starts to slow everything down.&nbsp;And in today’s&nbsp;environment,&nbsp;that&nbsp;kind of&nbsp;drag&nbsp;carries real risk.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>$4.4 Million.</h3>

<p>This is the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.concur.com/resource-center/reports/cfo-digital-risk-strategy">average global cost of a data breach</a>&nbsp;reached in 2025, highlighting how quickly gaps in visibility can translate into financial and reputational impact.&nbsp;</p>

<p lang="EN-US">Eventually, you realize&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;not just operational friction anymore.&nbsp;It’s&nbsp;coming from the system itself.&nbsp;</p>

<p>More than&nbsp;<a href="https://www.concur.com/resource-center/reports/cfo-digital-risk-strategy">half of finance leaders</a>&nbsp;now cite&nbsp;data quality as a top challenge when controlling costs, a dramatic increase in just&nbsp;the last&nbsp;two years.&nbsp;</p>

<p>What makes this even more complex is that the problem&nbsp;isn’t&nbsp;always a lack of data.&nbsp;</p>

<p>It’s&nbsp;fragmentation.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Many organizations report having strong data foundations while also reporting poor data quality or integration issues.&nbsp;</p>

<h2>What Leading Organizations Are Doing Differently&nbsp;</h2>

<p>If you look closely, the organizations&nbsp;that are&nbsp;gaining trust&nbsp;aren’t&nbsp;necessarily working harder.&nbsp;They’re&nbsp;working differently.&nbsp;</p>

<p>They’ve&nbsp;reduced the number of steps between:&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>A question and a confident answer&nbsp;</li>
	<li>A transaction and a clear explanation&nbsp;</li>
	<li>A report and a trusted&nbsp;source of truth&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<p>This&nbsp;isn’t&nbsp;about producing more reports.&nbsp;It’s&nbsp;about removing uncertainty from the ones that already exist. So,&nbsp;instead of...&nbsp;</p>

<ol>
	<li>Chasing documentation, they have it connected.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Validating&nbsp;after&nbsp;the transaction occurs, they enforce policies in real time.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Preparing for audits, they&nbsp;operate&nbsp;in a way that is already&nbsp;audit-ready.&nbsp;</li>
</ol>

<p>The impact is tangible&nbsp;results.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Modern reporting and AP systems, for example, enable real-time visibility, stronger compliance, and faster decision-making,&nbsp;without increasing the manual workload.&nbsp;</p>

<h2>A Different Way to Think About Transparency&nbsp;</h2>

<p>Any time change is&nbsp;rampant,&nbsp;it always helps&nbsp;us&nbsp;to zoom out.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Here, at SAP Concur, we like to think&nbsp;that transparency&nbsp;isn’t&nbsp;just a compliance requirement.&nbsp;It’s&nbsp;a design decision.&nbsp;</p>

<p>It shows up in how:&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>Data is structured.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Systems connect.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Workflows guide behavior.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Quick&nbsp;information can be accessed and understood.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<p>When those elements are aligned, transparency becomes less of a task and more of a byproduct.&nbsp;And&nbsp;that’s&nbsp;a win-win for everyone involved.&nbsp;</p>

<h2>What Trust Looks Like in Practice&nbsp;</h2>

<p>You can recognize it&nbsp;immediately.&nbsp;An auditor asks a&nbsp;question,&nbsp;and the answer is already available.&nbsp;A leadership team reviews spend, and understands not just the numbers, but the context behind them.&nbsp;</p>

<p>A public report is&nbsp;released and&nbsp;doesn’t&nbsp;require explanation.&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>No scrambling&nbsp;</li>
	<li>No stitching together data&nbsp;</li>
	<li>No second-guessing&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<p>Just clarity.&nbsp;</p>

<p>In high-stakes environments, that clarity creates space for teams to focus on their real work.&nbsp;</p>

<p>At Coulson Aviation, real-time expense capture and automated workflows allow crews to stay organized without slowing down operations.&nbsp;</p>

<blockquote>
<p><em>“Being able to upload receipts in real-time helps&nbsp;our teams stay organized and focus on their real job—putting out fires.”&nbsp;</em></p>
</blockquote>

<h2>Looking Ahead&nbsp;in Regulated Industries&nbsp;</h2>

<p>The expectations around transparency&nbsp;aren’t&nbsp;slowing down. If anything,&nbsp;they’re&nbsp;becoming more embedded in how regulated industries and other highly&nbsp;governed&nbsp;organizations are evaluated, funded, and trusted.&nbsp;</p>

<p>And&nbsp;perhaps most&nbsp;importantly, organizations that build this foundation&nbsp;aren’t&nbsp;just solving today’s pressure –&nbsp;they’re&nbsp;preparing for what comes next.&nbsp;</p>

<p>So,&nbsp;the question becomes less about readiness and more about design:&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li><strong>Are your systems built for explanation?&nbsp;</strong></li>
	<li><strong>Or are they built for reconstruction?&nbsp;</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>With a fully integrated, data-driven finance function, organizations like Coulson Aviation can scale operations while&nbsp;maintaining&nbsp;control, visibility, and speed without reintroducing complexity.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Because&nbsp;behind every report, every audit,&nbsp;and&nbsp;every request – there are people.&nbsp;</p>

<p>People who want to do the right thing.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Who&nbsp;care&nbsp;about&nbsp;accuracy.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Who&nbsp;take&nbsp;pride in&nbsp;stewardship.&nbsp;</p>

<p>The goal&nbsp;isn’t&nbsp;to ask more of them.&nbsp;It’s&nbsp;to give them systems that match the expectations placed on them.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Because when transparency is built in, trust&nbsp;doesn’t&nbsp;have to be defended.&nbsp;</p>

<p>It’s&nbsp;already there.&nbsp;</p>
