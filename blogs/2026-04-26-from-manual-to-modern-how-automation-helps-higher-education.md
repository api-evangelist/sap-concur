<p>Colleges and universities have a responsibility that goes beyond educating students. They must also safeguard the financial integrity of their institutions.&nbsp;Yet for&nbsp;many, outdated systems and manual processes make this increasingly difficult. Whether&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;managing travel and expense (T&amp;E) reports, auditing grant-related spend, or reconciling vendor invoices, compliance gaps can quietly erode budgets, reputations, and even access to critical funding.&nbsp;</p>

<p>According to SAP Concur research,&nbsp;<a href="https://www.concur.com/en-us/resource-center/brochures/better-audits-better-results-3-ways-control-spend-and-create-culture-compliance-across-your-campus">63% of organizations say time wasted during expense audits due to non-compliance is a major challenge</a>. And&nbsp;<a href="https://www.concur.com/en-us/resource-center/reports/wakefield-2024-report-higher-education-and-research-centers-look-technology-control-expenses-accelerate-innovation">data from Wakefield Research</a>&nbsp;shows higher education organizations face the same strain:&nbsp;nearly two-thirds&nbsp;(64%) take more than&nbsp;60 days&nbsp;to process vendor payments, and 37% of expense reports are still audited manually. For colleges and universities managing tight budgets, such inefficiencies are not only&nbsp;costly but&nbsp;also expose significant compliance risks.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Why compliance challenges are growing on campus&nbsp;</h3>

<p>Higher education faces unique financial and regulatory pressures that make compliance particularly complex:&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li><strong>Multiple funding sources</strong>&nbsp;— colleges and universities must track spending across federal, state, private, and grant-funded programs, each with its own reporting requirements and restrictions.&nbsp;</li>
	<li><strong>Decentralized spending</strong>&nbsp;— Faculty, researchers, and staff make independent purchasing and travel decisions, creating inconsistent oversight and policy enforcement.&nbsp;</li>
	<li><strong>Manual audits</strong>&nbsp;— Relying on spreadsheets and internal reviews means compliance issues often go unnoticed until after reimbursement — when&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;too late.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<p>These challenges increase risk and make it harder for universities to deliver on their core mission. Non-compliance can threaten funding, damage institutional reputation, and reduce the resources available for student success.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Automation brings visibility, control, and consistency&nbsp;</h3>

<p>Manual processes may feel familiar, but they&nbsp;can’t&nbsp;provide the real-time&nbsp;visibility&nbsp;today’s higher education organizations need. According to Wakefield Research,&nbsp;<a href="https://www.concur.com/en-us/resource-center/reports/wakefield-2024-report-higher-education-and-research-centers-look-technology-control-expenses-accelerate-innovation">46% of higher ed leaders say automated compliance checks are essential for identifying fraud and out-of-policy spend before it happens</a>, not after.&nbsp;</p>

<p>By digitizing travel and expense management, universities can:&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>Identify&nbsp;compliance issues early with intelligent audit tools that automatically flag out-of-policy transactions.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Reduce manual workload by automating routine reviews and&nbsp;focusing&nbsp;auditors on high-risk reports.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Preserve institutional knowledge through centralized data that&nbsp;doesn’t&nbsp;disappear with staff turnover.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Protect sensitive data by consolidating spend management into secure, integrated platforms.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<p>A modern, integrated T&amp;E system can also help ensure compliance checks happen before payment or reimbursement, significantly reducing audit findings and protecting limited budgets.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>How intelligent technology transforms audits&nbsp;</h3>

<p>Automated audit tools combine the speed of AI with the accuracy of human&nbsp;expertise&nbsp;to strengthen financial control across&nbsp;campus. Benefits include the ability to:&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>Review expense reports and receipts before reimbursement,&nbsp;identifying&nbsp;waste, fraud, or misuse.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Use pattern analysis to uncover repeat offenders or systemic compliance risks.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Complete reviews in as little as&nbsp;48 hours, helping staff and vendors get paid faster.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<p>This intelligent, proactive approach allows universities to refine policies in real time and focus on continuous improvement instead of damage control.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Real-world example: How Michigan State University built smarter, safer travel compliance&nbsp;</h3>

<p>Michigan State University knows firsthand that compliance&nbsp;can’t&nbsp;be an afterthought. With faculty, staff, and students traveling around the globe for research, recruiting, and conferences, the university needed a way to manage thousands of travel requests while keeping visibility, safety, and policy&nbsp;adherence&nbsp;front and center.&nbsp;</p>

<p>When the travel team began re-evaluating its systems, the goal&nbsp;wasn’t&nbsp;just faster approvals. It was consistent compliance and&nbsp;stronger&nbsp;duty of care. According to Wakefield Research,&nbsp;<a href="https://www.concur.com/en-us/resource-center/reports/wakefield-2024-report-higher-education-and-research-centers-look-technology-control-expenses-accelerate-innovation">nearly half (48%) of higher education leaders require integration with existing finance and ERP systems, and 46% require automated internal compliance checks to flag fraud or noncompliant charges</a>. Michigan State University’s priorities reflected those same needs.&nbsp;</p>

<p>“We were looking for a partner that operated with other partners to create that really holistic, comprehensive duty of care product,” said Ed Phillips, University Travel Manager. “By connecting Concur,&nbsp;TripLink, and our ERP, we built a system that’s user-friendly, auditable, and compliant.”&nbsp;</p>

<p>Now, Michigan State University can automatically capture travel data from booking through reimbursement, integrate with its duty of care partner to&nbsp;locate&nbsp;travelers in emergencies, and&nbsp;maintain&nbsp;consistent visibility into spend across departments — a significant leap from the fragmented, manual processes of the past.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Michigan State University’s journey reflects what many colleges and universities are realizing: modern T&amp;E systems are more than operational upgrades.&nbsp;They’re&nbsp;compliance enablers. With 83% of higher education organizations planning to invest in new or updated travel and expense technology this year, forward-thinking schools are following this lead to&nbsp;protect&nbsp;their people, their data, and their funding.&nbsp;</p>
